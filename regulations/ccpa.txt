California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) - California Privacy Law

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) grants California residents specific rights regarding their personal information and imposes obligations on businesses that collect, use, or sell personal information.

Key Requirements:
- Right to know what personal information is collected
- Right to know whether personal information is sold or disclosed
- Right to say no to the sale of personal information
- Right to access personal information
- Right to equal service and price (non-discrimination)
- Right to delete personal information
- Notice at collection of personal information
- Opt-out mechanisms for data sales

Relevant for features involving:
- Personal data collection and processing
- Data sales or sharing with third parties
- User data access and control features
- Privacy settings and preferences
- Data deletion and account management
- Targeted advertising and profiling
- User consent and opt-out mechanisms
- Data transparency and disclosure

Compliance Requirements:
- Provide notice of data collection practices
- Implement data access and deletion mechanisms
- Enable opt-out from data sales
- Maintain non-discrimination policies
- Support data portability requests
- Implement reasonable security measures
- Provide clear privacy notices
- Enable user control over personal information
